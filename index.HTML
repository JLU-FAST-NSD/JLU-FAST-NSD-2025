About the Event

"Celebrating scientific breakthroughs and innovation"

Hosted by the Faculty of Science & Technology, Jagran Lakecity University

National Science Day is celebrated every February 28 in India to mark the discovery of the Raman Effect by Sir C.V. Raman, the first Indian to win the Nobel Prize. This day is dedicated to spreading awareness about the importance of science, its applications, and its impact on society.

The event, themed "Empowering Indian Youth for Global Leadership in Science and Innovation for Vikshit Bharat", aims to ignite curiosity, encourage innovation, and promote a scientific temper among the youth. It features a dynamic blend of interactive sessions and competitions, designed to inspire future generations.

Scientific Model Competition

"Showcasing innovation & creative science"

Participants will compete in various categories tailored to different academic levels: Class 6-8, Class 9-12, and Under Graduate. Each team (2 to 4 members) is encouraged to explore themes ranging from Science for Society to Space and Astronomy, Technology & Engineering, and Environmental Science.

The competition is designed to inspire creative thinking and innovative solutions to modern challenges, making science accessible and engaging.

Science Day Lecture

"Inspiration from leading experts"

The keynote lecture will be delivered by Dr. Punit Swaroop, a prominent Scientist and Engineer from IIRS, ISRO. His talk will delve into the importance of scientific inquiry and its transformative impact on society.

This session is designed to enlighten, motivate, and instill a passion for science among the participants.
